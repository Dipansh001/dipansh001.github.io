SQL Case Study: Subscription Billing System

Problem

Subscription-based businesses require a reliable way to manage customers, plans, billing cycles, and payments.

Poorly designed systems often lead to duplicate charges, inconsistent records, and revenue leakage.

Requirements

  • Store customer and subscription data
  • Support multiple subscription plans
  • Track billing cycles and payment status
  • Prevent duplicate or invalid billing entries

Design

The database is designed using normalized relational tables separating customers, plans, subscriptions, and payments.

Foreign key constraints ensure referential integrity, while data constraints prevent invalid states.

Logic

Business rules such as billing frequency and payment validation are enforced at the database level.

Stored procedures and triggers automate recurring actions and reduce the risk of human error.

Analysis

This schema enables analysis of active subscriptions, revenue trends, failed payments, and churn indicators.

Trade-offs

Advanced billing scenarios such as proration, discounts, or multi-currency support are intentionally excluded to keep the system simple and reliable.
